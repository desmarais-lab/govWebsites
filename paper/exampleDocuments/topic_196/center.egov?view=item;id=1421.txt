September 12, 2016 in the Council Chambers of City Hall located at 610 Main Street.

APPROVAL OF THE MINUTES
The Board members examined the minutes of the regular meeting that was held on August 8,
2016. Secretary Krodel motioned and Vice Chairman Schmidt seconded to approve the minutes
as presented. Motion carried 3-0.
CHANGE ORDER
City Engineer Chad Hurm presented information on a change order for the Worrell Drive
project. Chad Hurm stated because of wider trenches being required additional flowable fill and
asphalt was required on this project. In turn Quality Craft Construction is requesting a change
order for the amount of $20,519.98.
Secretary Krodel motioned to approve the change order as presented for the amount of
$20,519.98. Vice Chairman Schmidt seconded. Motion passed 3-0.
CHANGE ORDER
City Engineer Chad Hurm presented information on a change order for the Golden Acers Phase
II project. Chad Hurm stated because of a stormwater pipe that was being replaced was outside
the easement and located next to a few trees that would have been removed if pipe was removed
the contractor pipe-filled the old stormwater pipe and left the pipe in the ground. In turn Love
Excavating is requesting a change order for the amount of $20,441.90.
Vice Chairman Schmidt motioned to approve the change order as presented for the amount of
$20,441.90. Secretary Krodel seconded. Motion passed 3-0.

STORMWATER DEPARTMENT REPORTS by City Engineer Chad Hurm
Carney Jasper LLC Parking Lot
City Engineer Chad Hurm stated that the Jasper Street
Department had the Stormwater Inspection Crew camera the stormsewer line in Carney Jasper
LLC parking lot. The inspection determined that the stormsewer pipe was compressed and
causing a dip on the surface of the parking lot. Chad Hurm stated that he has communicated
with Carney Jasper LLC and stated to them that the parking lot and stormsewer were private and
not owned by the City of Jasper.
Stormwater Funding and Projects for Rest of 2016 City Engineer Chad Hurm stated that
with the other stormwater projects finishing up that he believes there will be enough money left
in the 2016 stormwater budget to complete two smaller stormwater projects. Chad Hurm stated
that there is just above $100,000 left in the budget. He would like to look into two lining
projects.

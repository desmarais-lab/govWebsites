****** Board_of_Works_&_Safety_&#8211;_August_21,_2013 ******
    * Published on August 21, 2013
    * under Board_of_Works, News

The Board of Works and Safety for the City of Attica met, pursuant to Public
Notice, on Tuesday, August 21, 2013 at 12:00 P.M. in the City Council Room.
Present were Board Member Dave Huckleberry, City Clerk, Susan Stoll, Mayor, Bob
Shepherd and City Attorney, Tom O’Connor.
**** APPROVAL OF MINUTES ****
The minutes from the August 6, 2013 meeting were presented. Mr. Huckleberry
made a motion to pass them, Mayor Shepherd seconded. All were in favor and the
motion passed.
**** CITIZENS PETITIONS ****
There were no citizen petitions to be presented to the Board.
**** DEPARTMENT HEADS REPORTS ****
Mr. Jean was present but did not have anything to report.
Chief Cole asked Tom O’Connor if Kyle Askren could use comp time for the
hours he will be out due to knee surgery. Mr. O’Connor said that would be
acceptable. Chief Cole said he is looking into a quote from Rahm Tech for a new
computer as his is becoming incompatible with the other computers in the police
department. Chief Cole also reported he received a $1000 mini grant from Wal
Mart.
Mr. O’Farrell and Mr. Dismore were not present.
**** UNFINISHED BUSINESS ****
Mayor Shepherd and Mr. O’Connor reported that a letter was sent to the owner
of 802 Taylor asking for action on their run down property.
Mr. Huckleberry asked if Kyle Knosp had a current permit for the work he is
still doing on his property. He is currently converting the back porch into
living space. Mayor Shepherd said he would have Mr. Dismore look into the
status of the work and the permit.
There was only one bid submitted by today’s deadline for the demolition of
207 Perry St. That bid was submitted by Bienenman Construction. Mr. Huckleberry
asked if a motion needed to be made to accept the bid, Mr. O’Connor reported
that he would need to draft a contract for the work that is to be done.
**** NEW BUSINESS ****
A citizen asked if they run a business out their home, did they need to apply
for rezoning of their property. If the owner of the home meets the definition
of home occupancy, they can run a business out of their home. Some examples of
businesses that can be run out of the home are a beauty shop, a consultant, a
travel agency, babysitting, lawyers.
**** CLAIMS ****
Claims were presented for approval for payment. Mr. Huckleberry made a motion
to approve the claims. Mr. Shepherd seconded and the motion was passed.
**** MISCELLANEOUS BUSINESS ****
There was no miscellaneous business discussed.
**** ADJOURNMENT ****
With no further business to discuss, Mr. Huckleberry made a motion to adjourn.
Mayor Shepherd seconded. All were in favor and the motion passed.
